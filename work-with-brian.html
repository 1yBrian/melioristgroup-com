The founder

A practitioner,
not a theorist.

Brian has spent his career at the intersection of philosophy and practice — advising business owners, training practitioners, and writing about what it actually means to do work that counts.

He is a Meliorist: someone who believes the world gets better through constructive stewardship, wise action, better systems, and collaborative agency. Not through performance. Not through consumption of frameworks. Through removal of what's in the way.

He works with people who are already good at what they do and want to stop getting in their own way.

Limited Engagements

Targeted, practical, and grounded in what's actually happening in your business

This is not coaching in the traditional sense. Brian works as an advisor — someone who looks at what you've built, identifies what is working but underused and what is creating friction but persisting anyway, and helps you remove the latter while protecting the former.

Engagements are structured around real decisions: pricing, positioning, how you spend your time, what you stop doing. The philosophy is always in the room, but it shows up through what actually changes — not through additional frameworks to carry.

Right fit

This work is for people who are
already good at what they do.

This tends to work well when…

You've been doing this long enough to have real results — and a few persistent frustrations. You suspect the obstacle is something you're doing rather than something you don't know yet. You want a practitioner-to-practitioner conversation, not a motivational speaker. You're willing to subtract things, not just add them.

Also a good fit if…

You're a trainer or coach moving into a more advisory role and want to position yourself differently. You have ADHD or think in non-linear ways and are tired of programs that weren't designed for your brain. You want your work to be philosophically coherent — not just tactically optimized.

Not the right fit: If you're looking for someone to build your business for you, install new systems, or generate motivation to keep going — this probably isn't it. The work here assumes you already have the engine. We're looking for what's clogging it.

How it starts

Four steps, in order

01

A real conversation

Not a discovery call. An actual conversation about what you're building, what's working, and what keeps recurring. No pitch on either side.

02

Clarity on fit

We both decide if this is a good use of our time. If it isn't, Brian will say so and point you toward something that is.

03

A defined engagement

Scope, format, timeline, what done looks like. Not open-ended. Not indefinite. Structured around real decisions you need to make.

04

The work

Actual engagement: sessions, observations, honest feedback, targeted removal. The goal is a measurably clearer path — not more to carry.
